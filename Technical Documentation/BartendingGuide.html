Introduction

Over a decade spent behind the bar has given me some knowledge that I would like to pass on to those just starting out.

The hours can be a drag and dealing with intoxicated people is always a pain

Your feet will hurt, you will be sticky more often than you would like to think and you're definitely not going to get rich

But it has been an experience that I wouldn't trade for anything, even if I do hope that I have spent my last shift getting you heathens drunk

Skip The Academy

There's no need to spend money to learn recipes that you can teach yourself. No matter how expensive or well equipped a bartending academy may be, there is still no substitute for relevant restaurant experience.

If you know that bartending is what you want to do but have no experience, seek out a serving job at a busy restaurant.

Restaurant bartending is usually less demanding and restaurants will often look to promote servers and train them into bartenders

Prepare To Struggle

You're going to struggle. There are recipes to learn, new prep and cleaning to take care of and there are still those pesky customers always asking for something.

Ask Questions

Everyone was a beginner at some point. I've lost count of the number of times I learned something new from someone with less experience than me.

You will work with countless individuals and each one of them brings the experiences they have gained from working with other professionals.

The Basics

Learn the drinks that you will be making the most

Here is a simple Margarita recipe to get you started

Scratch Margarita
1.5 oz Tequila
.5 oz Triple Sec
4 oz Fresh Lime Juice
.25 oz Agave Nectar
  1. Always squeeze your lime juice fresh
  2. Combine ingredients in shaker with ice
  3. Shake well
  4. Strain over ice
  5. Garnish with lime wedge or wheel

Salted glass rim is always optional

If you want a sweeter margarita, you can add more Agave nectar.

Tequila not your thing? Maybe you're more of an old fashioned type

Old Fashioned 2 oz Bourbon or Whiskey
1 tsp Demerra Sugar
1 Orange Peel
1 Maraschino Cherry
  • Combine sugar, orange peel and cherry in glass
  • Gently muddle in glass
  • Add ice and chosen spirit
  • Gently stir ingredients with ice

Here are a few more common cocktails to help get you started

Mojito
1.5 oz White Rum
1 tbsp Demera sugar
1 oz Fresh Lime Juice
2 Sprigs of Mint
2 oz Soda Water
  • Muddle mint, sugar and lime juice together
  • Add rum and ice
  • Shake well and strain over fresh ice
  • Top with soda water and garnish with lime

Tip: if you don't like mint in your teeth, use a fine strainer. The mint flavor will still come through.
